MACE Liquid Water — Results & Visualizations

User-friendly summary of the MACE training run on the liquid water dataset (BingqingCheng/ab-initio-thermodynamics-of-water).

What the output means

  • Training: MACE (a machine-learning potential) was trained to predict energy and forces of bulk liquid water from atomic positions (H and O). The reference data are DFT energies and forces.
  • Validation metrics: After each epoch, the model is evaluated on a held-out validation set. We report:
    • Loss: Combined error the optimizer minimizes (energy and force terms). Lower is better.
    • MAE energy per atom (meV): Mean absolute error in predicted energy per atom. 1 meV = 0.001 eV. Lower is better.
    • MAE forces (meV/Å): Mean absolute error in predicted forces on atoms. Important for molecular dynamics. Lower is better.
  • Findings: Forces typically improve quickly; energy per atom can be noisier early in training. Longer training (e.g. 800 epochs as in the MACE docs) improves both.

  • Oxygen (O) is shown in red; hydrogen (H) in light blue.
  • Dotted lines indicate approximate O–H bonds (pairs within ~1.2 Å).
